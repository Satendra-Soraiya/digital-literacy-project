Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details through fake emails or websites.

For example, a student may receive an email claiming to offer a paid internship opportunity. The email looks professional and includes a link to apply. When the student clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that asks for login credentials.

Without verifying the authenticity, the student enters their details. As a result, the attacker gains access to their account and may misuse the information.

Students are often targeted because they actively search for opportunities and may act quickly without verifying sources. The consequences include account hacking, data theft, and financial loss.
